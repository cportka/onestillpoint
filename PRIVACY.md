# Privacy Policy

*Last updated: when the stars last aligned (2026). Short version: we don't want
your data, and we couldn't sell it even if we tried.*

One Still Point is a tiny indie art toy that draws a black hole in your browser.
It is not a business plan with a telescope. Here is the entire, honest story of
what happens to "your data."

## What we collect

Nothing. There is no account, no login, no email box begging for your address, no
analytics, no tracking pixels, no cookies following you around the cosmos, and no
server quietly taking notes. The whole thing runs on your device.

## What we store

Your **settings** — which sky you picked, how bright the disk is, whether you like
the FPS counter, that sort of thing — are saved in your browser's `localStorage`
so the app looks the way you left it next time. That data lives **on your
computer**, in your browser, under your control. We never see it. It never leaves
your machine. It is, frankly, none of our business.

Clear your browser storage (or open a private window) and it's gone — like it was
never written across the event horizon at all.

## What we share

With third parties? Nothing — there are no third parties. We have no servers to
leak from, no database to breach, and no marketing department to disappoint. If
you click a donation link, you'll go visit GitHub / Venmo / a blockchain, and
*their* privacy policies apply over there, not ours.

## The "do we need a consent banner" question

No. We are not tracking you, profiling you, or shipping your data anywhere, so
there is gloriously nothing to consent to. Enjoy the lack of a pop-up. You're
welcome.

## Your rights

You already have them all, because the data never left your possession. Want to be
forgotten? Clear your browser storage. Done. No support ticket, no 30-day waiting
period, no hold music.

## Questions

Open an issue on [GitHub](https://github.com/cportka/onestillpoint). We'll
probably be staring at the disk.
