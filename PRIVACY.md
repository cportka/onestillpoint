# Privacy Policy

*Short version: we don't sell or use your data (your data here is worthless anyway).*

One Still Point is a tiny indie art project that simulates astrophysics in your browser.
It is not a business plan with a telescope. Here is the story of
what happens to "your data" ...

## What we collect

Nothing. There is no account, no login, no email, no
analytics, no tracking pixels, no cookies and no
server taking notes. The whole thing runs on your device.

## What we store

Your **settings** — which sky you picked, how bright the disk is, whether you like
the FPS counter, etc — are saved in your browser's `localStorage`
so the app looks the way you left it next time. That data lives **on your
computer**, in your browser, under your control. No one else ever sees it. It never leaves
your machine.

Clear your browser storage (or open a private window) and all the data is gone.

## What we share

Nothing — there are no third parties. We have no servers and no marketing department to disappoint. If
you click a donation link, you'll go visit GitHub / Venmo / a blockchain, and
*their* privacy policies apply over there.

## The "do we need a consent banner" question

No. We are not tracking you, profiling you, or shipping your data anywhere so
there is nothing to consent to. Enjoy the lack of pop-up.

## Your rights

You already have them or not, we don't know.  We have no power in whether your rights are upheld properly in a just court of law.  But if you want to be
forgotten by this app just clear your browser storage.

## Questions
