# The intro — visual description (reality)

The companion to [`intro-script.md`](./intro-script.md): that file is the *ideal*
we're aiming for, this one is the *reality* — what the load intro actually looks
like in a given build, transcribed from a screen recording. Re-generate it
whenever the intro changes substantially, and always record the version.

> **Generated from:** `v0.13.0` — recording `OneStillPointAppIntroV0.13.mov`
> (640×480, default scene, default **Stars** background, **Physical** filter).

## What actually happens

1. **Open (~0–1 s).** The frame is nearly black over a dense, faintly-lensed star
   field; the camera sits well back. The shadow is only a soft dark smudge.
2. **The dolly (~1–4 s).** The camera slides inward — quick at first, then easing.
   As it closes, the background stars **stretch into curved arcs** sweeping around
   the centre: the gravitational "smoosh" is the most eye-catching early motion.
3. **Company arrives (~2–4 s).** A handful of **warm-orange and cool-blue points**
   (the prograde outer stars) fade up and slide across the frame; the dimmer inner
   planets follow, drifting the *other* way. They read as small bright travellers,
   not headliners — present from early on rather than a single late arrival.
4. **Ignition (~4–7 s).** A faint glow swells into a **bright white-grey accretion
   disk** wrapped around the dark circular shadow. The near edge and the
   lensed-over-the-top far edge close into the signature band, and the thin
   **photon ring** sharpens at the shadow's rim.
5. **Rest (~6.5 s onward).** The camera settles just above the disk plane on the
   bright, gently churning disk, framed by the lensed stars. Control returns to
   the user.

## Honest notes (reality vs. the script's ideal)

- The disk currently reads **near-white** — the default 15000 K temperature plus
  bloom washes the colour out; it's luminous rather than fiery. (The script imagines
  a more clearly hot-edged inner ring.)
- The orbiting bodies stay **small and subtle** through most of the dolly because
  the camera is far until ~5 s; they're visible but never dominate. (Open target in
  the script: camera-distance vs. body visibility.)
- The inner-edge **counter-clockwise sparkle** only becomes legible once the disk
  is fully formed and the camera is close — i.e. at the very end of the intro,
  not during it. (Open target in the script: pull the sparkle earlier.)
